Intolerance of Uncertainty and Indecisiveness Among University Students

Informed Consent Form

Dear participants,

You are invited to participate in a research study exploring The Relationship between Intolerance of Uncertainty and Indecisiveness Among University Students. Please read the information below carefully to understand the study's purpose, what your participation involves, and your rights as a participant.

Description of the study:

This study aims to examine the relationship between intolerance of uncertainty and indecisiveness among university students. Participants will complete a set of validated self-report questionnaires assessing intolerance of uncertainty, and a behavioural task for measuring indecisiveness. This study contributes by providing empirical evidence on the relationship between intolerance of uncertainty and indecisiveness among university students.

To take part in this study, you must:

  • Be aged between 18-30 years old
  • Be a current university student
  • Have normal color vision
  • Not have been diagnosed with psychological disorders such as OCD and anxiety disorder

What will be done:

In this form, you will be asked to complete:

  1. A demographic questionnaire
  2. The Intolerance of Uncertainty Scale (IU-12, 12 items)
  3. The Information Sampling Task

This entire study will take no longer than 30 minutes to complete.

Benefits of this study:

There will not be any direct benefit to you in participating in this study, but you will help the researcher to learn more about the relationship between intolerance of uncertainty and indecisiveness among university students in Malaysia.

Confidentiality:

Your participation in this study will remain completely confidential. No identifying information will be collected and your responses will be recorded anonymously. Only the research team and their academic supervisor will have access to the data, which will be used solely for research purposes. Every effort will be made to ensure your privacy is protected throughout the study.

Decision to quit at anytime:

Participation in this study is completely voluntary. If you choose to participate, you can withdraw at any time by simply closing this form without submitting it. There will be no consequences for choosing to withdraw from the study.

Information Sampling Task

In this task, you will see a grid of grey boxes. Behind each box is either a RED or BLUE color.

Your goal is to decide which color is more common in the grid. You can click on boxes to reveal their color. The more boxes you reveal, the more information you have to make your decision.

Instructions: Click boxes to reveal colors, then click "Make Decision" when you're ready to choose which color is more common.
